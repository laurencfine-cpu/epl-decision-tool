References & endnotes
  1. ACOG and Society of Family Planning recommend use of mifepristone for EPL, if available. The addition of mifepristone to misoprostol increases success rates and decreases time to completion in patients with missed EPL and anembryonic pregnancies.
  2. 61% overall (71% for incomplete EPL, 53% for empty sac, 35% for missed EPL). Casikar 2010.
  3. See Sonalkar 2020 and Schreiber 2018. Zhang 2005 found a 93% success rate with incomplete EPL using misoprostol-only.
  4. Prescribers and pharmacies must be REMS-certified; one prescriber can register for the group. See REMS 101. To find certified pharmacies stocking mifepristone and misoprostol: earlyoptionpill.com or genbiopro.com/prescribing.
  5. Timing of misoprostol should be determined by patient preference; many patients prefer to take it at home with easy access to a toilet. 2024 SFP EPL guidelines.
  6. Criteria based on ACOG guidelines. Updated criteria allow earlier diagnosis: CRL ≥5.3 mm, empty GS of 21 mm, and/or hCG rise <11% over baseline after 48 h (after US showing IUP of uncertain viability) are 100% diagnostic of nonviable pregnancy. 2024 SFP guidelines.
  7. Rh testing and RhoGAM are not recommended before 12 weeks' gestation for EPL and medical or procedural abortion (ACOG 2024, SFP 2022). No changes to ectopic pregnancy recommendations. Follow institutional guidance.
  8. Immediate IUD removal in pregnancy is standard of care. The IUD must be removed before giving mifepristone and misoprostol; if the patient chooses a procedure, it may be removed at the time of the procedure. ACOG Committee Opinion #672 (2016).
  9. ACOG recommends following hCGs until negative with PULs; use shared decision-making. ACOG Practice Bulletin #193.
  10. Rates of surgical procedure after expectant management vary widely; mean across studies 28%. Nanda 2012 (Cochrane).

Key references: Schreiber NEJM 2018; Sonalkar AJOG 2020; Zhang NEJM 2005; Casikar UOG 2010; Nanda Cochrane 2012; ACOG PB #193 & #200; SFP Clinical Recommendation 2024; Horvath Contraception 2022.
